# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Kresco serves three main user groups:

- Students who use the app to study, watch lessons, complete exercises, practice BAC exams, track progress, and message professors.
- Professors who manage learning support through their existing professor dashboard.
- Founders, operators, and support staff who run the business through admin and staff workflows.

The primary product context is authenticated task work. Users are trying to complete a concrete job, not browse a marketing site.

## Product Purpose

Kresco is a learning platform for structured academic practice and support. The product should make it easy for students to know what to do next, for professors to respond and guide, and for operators to understand business health and resolve operational work quickly.

Success means:

- Students find lessons, practice, exams, live sessions, and support without friction.
- Professors can act on student questions and learning needs without noise.
- Founders and operators can see growth, revenue, costs, messages, student status, and manual payment operations at a glance.

## Brand Personality

Confident, focused, supportive.

The product should feel modern and precise without becoming cold. Student-facing surfaces can feel a little more friendly and motivating. Admin surfaces should feel operational, restrained, and trustworthy.

## Anti-references

Avoid:

- Generic AI dashboard aesthetics: decorative gradients, glassmorphism, oversized cards, repeated icon-card grids, and empty visual noise.
- Marketing-page composition inside app workflows.
- Dense admin pages that become text spam instead of decision tools.
- Zero-value dashboards that look successful when real data failed to load.
- Reinvented form controls, strange custom interactions, or inconsistent button styles.
- Full-purple interfaces. Kresco purple is an accent, not the whole product.

## Design Principles

1. Surface the next useful action.
   Every screen should make the user's next step obvious without a paragraph of explanation.

2. Keep information high-signal.
   Remove filler labels, redundant subtitles, repeated summaries, and decorative metrics.

3. Separate learning energy from operator control.
   The student app can be warmer and more motivating. The admin workspace must be calmer, denser, and more analytical.

4. Use familiar product patterns.
   Tables, tabs, filters, segmented controls, date pickers, charts, sidebars, and forms should behave like established SaaS tools.

5. Earn every visual flourish.
   Motion, color, charts, and cards should clarify state or priority. If they do not help the task, remove them.

6. Make verification proportional to risk.
   Lightweight UI changes should not trigger expensive loops, but auth, payments, admin data, charts, forms, and shared primitives need stronger checks.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- Target WCAG AA contrast for text and controls.
- Respect reduced-motion preferences.
- Keep all controls keyboard accessible with visible focus states.
- Avoid relying on color alone for status.
- Student-facing pages should handle mobile widths carefully; admin pages should stay usable on laptop and desktop first, with functional responsive fallback.
